When Law Meets Lethal Force:

The US Police Shooting Story

Fatal incidents involving law enforcement officers in the United States are a significant concern. Congress requested data on police use of force in 1994, but it was never compiled, and the FBI doesn't collect this information. Occurances by City

The data reveals the most prominent cities in terms of incidents, with Los Angeles leading the list, experiencing a staggering 78 occurrences. Phoenix follows closely behind with 67 incidents. Additionally, Houston, Las Vegas, San Antonio, and Jacksonville have encountered 50, 46, 42, and 37 incidents, respectively. Interestingly, both Chicago and Columbus share the same count of 36 occurrences.

Victim Analysis

The distribution of victims' ages exhibits a pronounced right-skew, with a predominant age range spanning from 20 to 40 years. Additionally, there is a notable presence of older victims, reflecting a diverse age range.

Male victims significantly outnumber their female counterparts, constituting a staggering 94.97% of the total victims, totaling 4,815 cases. Conversely, female victims account for a smaller proportion, with a representation of 5.03%, encompassing 255 cases.

Among the victims, the majority are of White ethnicity, comprising 50.95% of the total victims, amounting to 2,583 individuals. In contrast, Black victims make up a substantial portion as well, representing 26.73% of the total victims, with 1,355 recorded cases.

Weapons Used

The weapon of choice for the majority of victims in these incidents was a gun, accounting for a substantial 2,987 cases. Additionally, a significant number of victims resorted to using a knife as their weapon, tallying 767 instances.

In 378 recorded instances, victims were found to be unarmed during these incidents. Furthermore, other weapons such as toy weapons, vehicles, and machetes were also employed, with frequencies of 196, 173, and 42 occurrences, respectively. Notably, there were 52 cases where the weapon used remained unidentified.

Occurances Timeline

Over time, there has been a significant decline in these occurrences, with the highest number of cases documented in 2015 at a staggering 913, while in 2022, the count has plummeted to a mere 84 cases. This substantial reduction could be attributed to various factors such as improved law enforcement training, increased public awareness, or changes in policing policies.
